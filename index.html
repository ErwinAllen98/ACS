Adjusted, Multipliable Risk Factors

The risk factors in the "History & Risks" tab are derived from the landmark INTERHEART study. This was a global case-control study that used multivariate logistic regression to isolate the independent impact of each risk factor. The published Adjusted Odds Ratios (AORs) were converted to Adjusted Likelihood Ratios (LRs) using a precise mathematical formula.

Key Principle: Because these LRs are statistically independent, they can be multiplied together to calculate a cumulative risk, providing a more accurate assessment than using single, unadjusted factors.

Other Clinical Findings & Prevalence

LRs for pain characteristics, physical findings, and ECG findings are primarily sourced from systematic reviews and meta-analyses, including the JAMA "Rational Clinical Examination" series and syntheses from TheNNT.com. Prevalence estimates are derived from a synthesis of epidemiological studies reflecting spectrum bias.
